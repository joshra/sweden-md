# Resources

> **30-second overview:** The resources section explains which sources Sweden.md should trust first, what each source type is good for, and how evidence should be organised before an article is written.

## Why it matters

Content quality is often decided before writing begins, at the moment sources are chosen. If the source layer is weak, the article will usually drift into vagueness, repetition, or second-hand summary. The resources section exists to prevent that.

## What this section is for

It establishes source priority, clarifies which institutions are useful for which kinds of questions, and prevents Sweden.md from becoming a random collection of external links.

## How to read this section

Think of it in two layers: official entry points for institutions and governance, and research/statistical institutions for deeper verification and evidence.

## Contemporary significance

In an AI-assisted writing environment, source governance becomes part of editorial quality. The resources section makes that governance visible.
