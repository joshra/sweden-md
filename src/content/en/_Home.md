# Sweden.md

> **30-second overview:** Sweden.md is not a travel notebook or a loose encyclopedia. It is a Markdown-first, editorially curated knowledge base about Sweden. Content comes first, and the site layer is built to consume and present that content.

## Why it matters

Many country-focused sites eventually become front-end page collections with hard-to-maintain copy and inconsistent standards. Sweden.md matters because it treats content as a durable asset that can be edited, linked, translated, and expanded independently of the interface.

That approach makes editorial consistency, cross-linking, and bilingual work easier to maintain over time.

## How to read this project

Sweden.md is organized around category hubs that lead into topic articles. Each article should answer a clear question, such as why an institution matters, how a cultural practice emerged, or how a historical period still shapes contemporary Sweden. Articles are connected through `[[wikilink]]` structure rather than standing alone.

The website is only the reading interface. The real source of truth is `knowledge/`, which is then projected into `src/content/` for the site to consume.

## Core principles

First, the project is curated rather than merely accumulative. Second, every article should follow a common structure, including a `30-second overview`, `Why it matters`, core sections, related or further reading, and references. Third, articles should prioritize credible sources, especially official institutions, statistics agencies, research organizations, and representative cultural bodies.

## Suggested starting points

- Start from the category hubs to understand the overall map.
- Read `History`, `Society`, and `Culture` first, then use `Cities` and `Geography` to build a spatial map before branching into `Food`, `Technology`, `Nature`, and other areas.
- To understand the writing model, begin with the materials in `About`, especially the editorial principles and article template.
