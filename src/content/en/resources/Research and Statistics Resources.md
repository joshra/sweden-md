# Research and Statistics Resources

> **30-second overview:** Research and statistics resources are Sweden.md's second source layer. They make it possible to support cultural, demographic, institutional, and historical claims with evidence rather than intuition.

## Why it matters

Without research and statistics, country writing often stays stuck at the level of reputation. Sweden.md needs these sources to test assumptions, support claims, and keep articles from leaning too heavily on general impressions.

## Key institutions and their uses

### Statistics Sweden (SCB)

Best for population, labor, households, education, regions, and other quantitative topics.

### Swedish National Heritage Board

Useful for cultural heritage, historical environments, preservation, and public memory topics.

### The Riksdag

Also important here because institutional topics often need direct access to legislative material and parliamentary process.

### Swedish Institute

Helpful when an article needs internationally oriented cultural framing or English-language explanation tied to Sweden's public diplomacy.

## How to use them well

The goal is not to decorate prose with numbers. The goal is to let evidence discipline the argument. If a trend, contrast, or institutional claim cannot be supported, the wording should be narrowed rather than exaggerated.
